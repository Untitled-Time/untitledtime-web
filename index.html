EST. 2026 // WILLIAM & MARY

Untitled Time

The Space Where the Tree Outlives the Rocket

01. THE DIAGNOSIS

The Crisis of Patience

It is February 8th, 2026. On Launch Pad 39B, the Artemis II rocket stands as a white obelisk against the Florida sky. It is built to escape.

Simultaneously, we have entered the "Post-Labor" era. Algorithms now code, diagnose, and create. In a world where work—the traditional source of human dignity—is gone, we are left with a haunting question: What do we do all day?

"If you do not need to survive, how do you find a reason to live?"
02. THE VESSEL

The Anti-Rocket

Silicon Valley is built on disruption. NASA is built on escape. Untitled Time is a proposal to build a "Vessel" at William & Mary—the Deep Time institution of the nation.

Drawing inspiration from the Future Library in Oslo, where spruce saplings grow for a century to become books no one living today will read, we propose a physical and intellectual space that projects humanity 100 years into the future.

We are asserting that in 2126, there will still be a "We" to read them.

03. THE INQUIRY

Design Phases

  • Phase I: The Anchor

    Confronting the Rocket (Escape) vs. The Algorithm (Obsolescence). What "Earthly Value" remains for us to preserve?

  • Phase II: The Form

    Engaging the College Woods (160+ year old trees) and Special Collections to design a vessel that embodies "Deep Time."

  • Phase III: The Framework

    Developing a "Centennial Trust" and a Trans-Atlantic study abroad program moving from Scotland's deep past to Norway's deep future.
