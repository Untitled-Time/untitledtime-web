01. THE EXIT

火 The White Obelisk

It is February 8th, 2026. On Launch Pad 39B, the Artemis II rocket stands as a white obelisk against the Florida sky. It is a machine built for a singular purpose: escape. We are building these ships because the human mandate has always been to expand, to push outward, to leave the cradle.

But while we build engines to leave Earth, a terrifying quiet is spreading across it.

We have entered the "Post-Labor" era. The predictions of Bletchley Park have arrived. Algorithms now code, diagnose, and create faster than any hand. In a world where work—the traditional source of human dignity—is rendered obsolete, we are left with a haunting question, echoing in the vacuum of our own making:

"If you do not need to survive, how do you find a reason to live?"
02. THE ANCHOR

木 The Anti-Rocket

Silicon Valley is built on disruption. NASA is built on escape. Untitled Time is a proposal to build an "Anchor" at William & Mary—the deep time institution of the nation.

Drawing inspiration from the Future Library in Oslo, where spruce saplings grow for a century to become books no one living today will read, we propose a physical and intellectual vessel that projects humanity 100 years into the future. It is a cathedral of patience in an age of acceleration.

We are asserting a radical hope: that in 2126, there will still be a "We" to read them. That the tree will outlive the rocket.

03. THE VOID

無 The Design of Stillness

How do we design for a century? We must move from the thermodynamics of combustion to the Taoist concept of Wu Wei (無為)—action through non-action.

  • Phase I: The Definition of Value

    Confronting the Rocket (Escape) vs. The Algorithm (Obsolescence). We must identify the "Earthly Values" that cannot be digitized, archived, or automated. What is the irreducible kernel of the human soul?

  • Phase II: The Vessel Form

    Engaging the College Woods—home to trees that have stood since the Civil War—and Special Collections to design a structure that breathes. It is not a warehouse for dead paper, but a living respiratory system for public thought.

  • Phase III: The Centennial Trust

    Developing a Trans-Atlantic pedagogical bridge, moving from Scotland's deep past to Norway's deep future, teaching the next generation to become stewards of a timeline they will not inhabit.
